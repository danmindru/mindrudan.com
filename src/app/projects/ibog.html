Introduction

This project covered the concept development and prototyping of an interactive reading web application. The product was targeted at children 7-12 years old.

Process & concept overview

The development started with a thorough target group analysis, followed by a style and color analysis. The outlines of the proposed concept are the following:

  • Simplify

    Reduce the amount of functionality and graphics to the minimum, focus on the content.

  • Strive for composition balance

    Discover the right combination between an easy to read and pleasantly scalable font. Place the content for readability, reduce UI complexity in favor of content clarity.

  • Embrace color

    Use color to build appealing scenes for the target group. Colors should be used to suggest the context and engage users into a interactive-discovery process.

  • Use reading as experience / Time as navigation

    Offer more than a tool for learning, offer a learning experience. Transitions, subtle movements, automatic context-aware and user type options are tools to build such an experience. The users should feel in control and be able to input changes.
    Use time to motivate and give a sense of control or task complexity.

  • Assist users with common challenges

    Basic tools will be there for the typical challenges met by the target audience. This will allow learning without external distractions. Rather than having to often stop and search for information, all the needed content will be present within the application.

  • Engage / Learn without pressure

    Allow users to skip tasks and choose what and when they want to do it.

  • Offer methods of expression

    Harvest the target group's creativity by allowing the manipulation of layouts, scenes and characters present in the text.

Simplify - the foundation of the concept

At its heart, the concept should offer a clean and straightforward reading experience. Reading is the most important element of the application. Therefore, this is where the concept starts to develop.

Color is one of the most important elements in the concept. While it offers surprise and excitement, it can also help with giving focus. Color schemes and combinations are carefully picked for the target audience.

Color combinations may occur on different keyword triggers. The key is, offer a balance of colors to extend and vary the experience (without making it predictable). The emotions generated play a key role in memorizing and understanding the content.

Assist users with common challenges

Understanding that the target group may have a limited vocabulary, it is easy to see that distraction when searching for unknown words will be a major obstacle. The solution is the following: users can (and are encourage to) explore each word and get an explanation in the context.

The explanation transitions from the bottom of the screen, accompanied by a graphical element to stimulate visual memory.

Displays with smaller canvases will show an adapted version of the explanation.

Engage / Learn without pressure

Each touch/click of a word, will be stored by the application. At key points of the reading (for example, quarter of the reading time, half of the reading time) users will be prompted with question of a certain number of the words they have interacted with.

These prompts can always be skipped by the users. They will have the choice to continue reading uninterrupted. The users are not forced to take an action, they are in control. However, if skipped, they will be displayed at the end of the lecture.

Offer methods of expression

The final addition to the experience is the word ‘inspecting’ mechanism. Every time a word is inspected, next to the pop-up the user may click/touch an 'addition' symbol (+).

By taking this action the content will be moved to background and the focus will change on the coloured canvas of the page. An object matching the inspected word will be created and added to the scene. The object can be manipulated (rotated, scaled, moved) and kept as part of the scene.

This functionality can be considered as being core of the concept by 3 measures.
Firstly, it will encourage users to click and discover definitions of many words in the text.
Secondly, it will help users establish visual relations between a definition and a graphical element.
Lastly, it will allow the users to exercise their creativity and build their own 'world' around the current literature.
This technique can be extended by allowing users to build characters in a fairytale or even offering creative mini-games.

tl, dr

The challenge behind this project was to create an interactive learning system for a demanding target group. It had to balance a content-first approach with a dynamic and engaging set of tools, while having a responsive layout.
